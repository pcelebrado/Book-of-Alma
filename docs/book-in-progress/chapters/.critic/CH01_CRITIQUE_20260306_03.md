## Findings

### HIGH: Correlation/Dispersion Claim Reversed (Macro vs Idiosyncratic)
- **Location**: C:/P4NTH30N/OP3NF1XER/nate-alma/book-in-progress/chapters/CH01.md:294
- **Issue**: The text states that when dispersion rises and correlation falls, it “may suggest that macro factors are dominating stock-specific factors.” This is directionally backwards.
- **Evidence**: “When individual stocks begin moving independently (dispersion rises) while index correlation falls, this may suggest that macro factors are dominating stock-specific factors.”
- **Required Fix**: Rewrite the correlation/dispersion paragraph to reflect correct mechanics: macro dominance typically increases correlation (and can suppress idiosyncratic dispersion), while dispersion rising with falling correlation implies idiosyncratic / single-name drivers (or sector rotation) dominating.
- **Bible Reference**: None required (market microstructure fact).
