## Findings

### HIGH: Missing mandatory risk disclosure
- **Location**: C:/P4NTH30N/OP3NF1XER/nate-alma/book-in-progress/chapters/CH01.md#L1
- **Issue**: Chapter presents trading implications and tactical guidance without explicit statement that options/derivatives carry a high level of risk and are not suitable for everyone.
- **Evidence**: "**Trading Implication:** ..." repeated throughout.
- **Required Fix**: Add an explicit risk disclosure section before the first tactical guidance (before section 1.1 or at the end of The Opening).

### HIGH: Unsourced, specific market-history assertions presented as fact
- **Location**: C:/P4NTH30N/OP3NF1XER/nate-alma/book-in-progress/chapters/CH01.md#L9
- **Issue**: Specific timeline/events and index levels are asserted without source notes or framing as hypothetical.
- **Evidence**: "In early February 2025... S&P 500 rallied nearly two hundred points..."; "algorithms... bought gamma at 6050... selling delta at 5950".
- **Required Fix**: Either (1) cite a source-note appendix with dates/levels and what data supports the claims, or (2) rewrite as a generic illustrative example with explicitly hypothetical labels.

### HIGH: Gamma/vanna causal claims are technically unsafe as written
- **Location**: C:/P4NTH30N/OP3NF1XER/nate-alma/book-in-progress/chapters/CH01.md#L47
- **Issue**: Treats "vanna positive" as intrinsically supportive and "vanna negative" as intrinsically suppressive, without defining vanna, without specifying vol change direction, and without acknowledging chain/tenor dependence.
- **Evidence**: "When gamma is short and vanna is positive, price becomes a voting machine..."; "When gamma flips long and vanna turns negative...".
- **Required Fix**: Add a precise definition block for gamma and vanna and rewrite these sentences to conditional, mechanism-correct language (e.g., specify vol up/down, dealer positioning sign, and that effects are local/tenor-dependent).

## Risk Issues

- Multiple "Trading Implication" directives are offered without explicit disclosure that trading derivatives involves a high level of risk, can result in significant loss, and is not suitable for everyone.
- Overstated flow-causality may cause readers to over-size or over-trust a single-variable explanation (gamma/vanna) for multi-week market moves.

## Blockers

1. Add explicit risk disclosure before tactical guidance.
2. Remove or source specific 2025 event/level claims.
3. Fix/qualify gamma-vanna mechanism statements with correct conditionality and definitions.
4. Remove or source Alma-phrased principles not traceable to the Bible corpus.
