## Findings

### HIGH: Short-Gamma Hedging Direction Wrong in High-Vol Case Study
- **Location**: C:/P4NTH30N/OP3NF1XER/nate-alma/book-in-progress/chapters/CH02.md:286
- **Issue**: In a selloff, a dealer who is *short gamma* typically sells into weakness (procyclical hedging), not “buys deltas as price falls.” This matters because it is the core mechanical claim of the case study.
- **Evidence**: “Dealers who were short gamma are now forced to buy deltas as price falls, exacerbating the move.”
- **Required Fix**: Correct the sentence to reflect procyclical hedging: short-gamma dealer hedging pressure is generally *sell as spot falls / buy as spot rises* (with explicit note that the sign depends on net positioning and which options are driving exposure).
- **Bible Reference**: None required for sign mechanics; this is basic dealer-hedging logic.

### MEDIUM: Case Studies Use “Dealers Are Short Gamma” as a Default State
- **Location**: C:/P4NTH30N/OP3NF1XER/nate-alma/book-in-progress/chapters/CH02.md:251
- **Issue**: Some case-study language presents dealer short gamma as a default condition rather than conditional on prevailing customer flow/inventory.
- **Evidence**: “Dealers are short gamma from the steady supply of hedging and yield-enhancement strategies.”
- **Required Fix**: Add one explicit conditional clause to keep it honest (e.g., “in this constructed example, assume net dealer gamma is short due to…”). Do not imply universality.
- **Bible Reference**: None.
