## Executive Summary

This chapter has the right intent (second-order Greeks as the "volatility lever" under spot) and it includes an explicit risk disclosure. It fails on integrity and pedagogy: it is far under-length relative to the 80k expansion gate, it attributes multiple claims to Alma without adequate Bible anchoring at the claim location, and its JPM collar case study cites a Bible file that is effectively empty in the local corpus capture (so the quoted lesson is not verifiable). There are also several places where vanna/speed sign and flow mapping are asserted too confidently without stating sign conventions and conditioning.

## Findings

### HIGH: Fails expansion gate for 80k manuscript target
- **Location**: OP3NF1XER/nate-alma/book-in-progress/chapters/CH05.md
- **Issue**: At 1890 words, the chapter is too thin to teach second-order Greeks safely. It needs additional definitions, sign conventions, and multiple worked examples.
- **Required Fix**: Expand by +3000 to +4500 words. Minimum deliverables: (1) sign-convention box for vanna and speed, (2) a simple scenario table (IV up/down x dealer vanna sign), (3) at least 2 Bible-anchored case studies from the Part-II CH05 corpus.

### HIGH: JPM collar case study is not verifiable from cited Bible file
- **Location**: OP3NF1XER/nate-alma/book-in-progress/chapters/CH05.md:49-59
- **Issue**: The chapter attributes a specific March 14, 2025 Alma comment about the collar being "minor impact" and warning against "furus". The cited Bible corpus file (`unknown-date-bottom-jpm-collar-opex-systematics-intraday-post-14-march.md`) contains only the post header and no such text in the local corpus capture.
- **Evidence**: The cited file is 16 lines and does not contain the quoted content.
- **Required Fix**: Replace this citation with a Bible source that actually contains the collar-warning language in the local corpus (e.g. `bible/corpus/substack/unknown-date-theta-decay-when-will-vol-come-back-intraday-post-30-june-wi.md`, which explicitly contains this warning), OR remove the quote and reframe as author synthesis.

### HIGH: Alma attribution discipline is inconsistent (claims without citations at point of use)
- **Location**: OP3NF1XER/nate-alma/book-in-progress/chapters/CH05.md:37-41, 51-54, 71-76
- **Issue**: The chapter repeatedly states "Alma's caution..." / "Alma repeatedly warns..." / "Alma's short-vanna commentary..." but does not anchor those specific claims with direct Bible citations where they appear.
- **Required Fix**: For every sentence that starts with (or implies) "Alma says/teaches/warns", add an inline Bible citation adjacent to the sentence OR rewrite as author synthesis.

### MEDIUM: Vanna and speed flow mapping needs sign conventions and explicit conditioning
- **Location**: OP3NF1XER/nate-alma/book-in-progress/chapters/CH05.md:69-79
- **Issue**: The text asserts directionality (e.g., IV falling in a short-vanna regime creates selling pressure) without stating the sign convention used for vanna, without specifying "dealer net" vs "street" vs "customer" positioning, and without specifying which part of the surface (ATM vs tails; front-end vs back months) dominates.
- **Required Fix**: Add a short "Sign and Scope" box: define vanna as ∂Δ/∂σ under a stated convention; then explain that the flow result depends on (a) dealer net vanna sign in the relevant zone/tenor, and (b) whether IV is rising/falling.
