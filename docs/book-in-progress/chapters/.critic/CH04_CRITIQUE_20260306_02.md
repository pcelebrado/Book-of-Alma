## Findings

### HIGH: Invented case-study content (trade construction) not supported by Bible source
- **Location**: OP3NF1XER/nate-alma/book-in-progress/chapters/CH04.md:370-399
- **Issue**: The chapter claims the March 26 post provides a "gamma-based trade construction" example and then supplies a multi-bullet recipe (cheap IV vs expected RV, dealer net short gamma, event risk, etc.). The cited Bible file as ingested is short and does not contain this trade-construction content. This is invented instruction presented under a Bible-anchored case study heading.
- **Evidence**: "While the full post is truncated, the title itself..." followed by: "Long gamma trades are typically constructed when...".
- **Required Fix**: Remove the March 26 case study as a Bible-anchored case study unless you can cite exact supporting text from the Bible corpus file. If you want to keep the educational recipe, reframe it explicitly as "author synthesis" and move it out of "Case Study" format (and do not imply Alma said it).

### HIGH: Incorrect mapping of "long vanna" to spot/vol correlation
- **Location**: OP3NF1XER/nate-alma/book-in-progress/chapters/CH04.md:130-136
- **Issue**: The chapter states "Long vanna: Positive correlation between spot and volatility, meaning vol expansion accompanied spot rallies". This is not a definition of vanna and is not generally true without specifying whose vanna, sign conventions, and the joint move (spot up/down, vol up/down). This can miseducate readers into using the wrong regime mapping.
- **Required Fix**: Either (1) remove these interpretive bullets entirely and keep only the Bible quote + a narrow paraphrase, or (2) rewrite with precise conditioning language (and ideally defer to CH05 for vanna details).

### MEDIUM: "Alma's teaching rejects..." phrasing implies doctrine without direct citation
- **Location**: OP3NF1XER/nate-alma/book-in-progress/chapters/CH04.md:185
- **Issue**: "Alma's teaching rejects that simplification" is a doctrine-style claim. If it is not a direct Alma statement in the Bible, it must be softened/reframed.
- **Required Fix**: Replace with author framing ("A common retail simplification is...", "In practice, pinning is conditional...") or cite an explicit Bible passage.
