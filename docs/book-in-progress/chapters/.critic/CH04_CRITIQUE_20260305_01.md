## Findings

### CRITICAL: Incorrect Black-Scholes gamma formula
- **Location**: C:/P4NTH30N/OP3NF1XER/nate-alma/book-in-progress/chapters/CH04.md#L49
- **Issue**: Formula is not the Black-Scholes gamma; it multiplies where it should divide and uses the wrong structure.
- **Evidence**: "Γ = e^(-rfτ) × S × σ × τ × n(d1)".
- **Required Fix**: Replace with a correct expression (e.g., for a non-dividend-paying asset: `Gamma = n(d1) / (S * sigma * sqrt(tau))`; for indices include dividend yield `q` as `e^{-q tau}` multiplier). If you cannot present it correctly and explain variables, remove the formula and keep the proportionality narrative.

### HIGH: Missing distinction between option gamma and dealer net gamma positioning
- **Location**: C:/P4NTH30N/OP3NF1XER/nate-alma/book-in-progress/chapters/CH04.md#L7
- **Issue**: The chapter blends the Greek definition with the market-structure concept "dealers long/short gamma" without explicitly separating them.
- **Evidence**: "Gamma is... forcing function" followed by hedging flow claims.
- **Required Fix**: Add a short clarification that "dealer is long/short gamma" refers to net exposure from customer positioning (aggregated), not a property of the underlying itself.

### HIGH: Numeric flip/pinning examples need explicit sourcing
- **Location**: C:/P4NTH30N/OP3NF1XER/nate-alma/book-in-progress/chapters/CH04.md#L72
- **Issue**: Specific levels and centroid values are stated as factual without citations.
- **Evidence**: 5890/5856 (and centroid 5965.94).
- **Required Fix**: Cite the exact Bible post(s) where these levels are published; if centroid cannot be sourced, remove it.

### MEDIUM: Overgeneralized claim about gamma increasing as sigma/time shrink
- **Location**: C:/P4NTH30N/OP3NF1XER/nate-alma/book-in-progress/chapters/CH04.md#L54
- **Issue**: True in the ATM neighborhood, but not a universal monotone claim across moneyness/tenor.
- **Evidence**: "As both time and volatility shrink, gamma increases".
- **Required Fix**: Add the condition (ATM/near-ATM, holding other inputs fixed) or rewrite as "gamma tends to concentrate near ATM into expiry".
