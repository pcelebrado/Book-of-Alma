## Findings

### HIGH: Signal-to-Action Mapping Contains Unsourced, Advice-Like Numeric Prescriptions
- **Location**: C:/P4NTH30N/OP3NF1XER/nate-alma/book-in-progress/chapters/CH10.md:133
- **Issue**: The chapter tells the reader to reduce exposures by specific percentages (10-20%, 15-25%, 25-40%, 30-50%) and to buy specific instruments “immediately.” This is presented as generalizable rather than illustrative, and most thresholds are not sourced.
- **Evidence**: “reduce equity beta by 10-20%…”, “Cut growth equity exposure by 15-25%…”, “Cut equity exposure by 25-40%…”, “reduce equity beta by 30-50%…”, “purchase VIX call spreads… immediately”.
- **Required Fix**: Rewrite 10.6 to (a) remove precise percentages OR (b) clearly label all numbers as illustrative examples with explicit account-dependence and non-advice framing. Convert “immediately” language to conditional response (“may consider”) unless the directive is tied to an Alma-sourced quote.

### HIGH: “Historically Precedes” Claims Need Sourcing or Softer Language
- **Location**: C:/P4NTH30N/OP3NF1XER/nate-alma/book-in-progress/chapters/CH10.md:39
- **Issue**: Statements like “MOVE above 120 has historically preceded…” and similar timing windows (“2-6 weeks”, “4-12 weeks”) are asserted without evidence.
- **Evidence**: “Readings above 120 have historically preceded equity volatility expansion.”
- **Required Fix**: Either cite a source, or rewrite as hypothesis/heuristic with uncertainty (e.g., “often coincides with” / “can be an early warning”).

## Risk Issues

- Risk disclosure exists, but the body text still reads like an implementation manual with concrete sizing changes. This increases reader-harm risk; fix by reframing as illustrative framework.

## Blockers

1. Remove/soften unsourced numeric prescriptions and deterministic language in 10.6.
2. Remove or soften “historically precedes / timing window” claims unless sourced.
